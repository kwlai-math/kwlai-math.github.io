I am a postdoc at the University of Bonn as part of the ERC Synergy grant HyperK. Previously, I was a visiting assistant professor at UMass Amherst. I obtained my PhD in 2018 from Brown University under the supervision of Brendan Hassett.

I am an algebraic geometer. Since I was a PhD student, I am interested in topics relevant to cubic fourfolds and K3 surfaces, with a special focus on the symmetries of their moduli spaces induced by lattice isometries, derived equivalences, and birational maps. During the years at UMass Amherst, I started to expend the scope of my research to algebraic geometry over finite fields and more. I am currently studying autoequivalences of K3 categories, bijective Cremona transformations, and Frobenius nonclassical hypersurfaces.
